Case Study · Kita TL;DR

From blind box to brochure — a non-profit advocacy toolkit that starts conversations.

A 12-week project for Kita, a Singapore non-profit supporting at-risk youth. Turned a traditional informational brochure into a collectible object advocates actually carry and use.

Role
Project Owner
Team
5, cross-functional
Timeline
12 weeks
Year
2024
Problem
Kita's advocates wanted to share its mission but existing brochures closed conversations rather than opened them. No budget for paid reach, no IPC status, and a hard constraint: no sob stories — they'd undermine Kita's own message of youth agency.
Solution
A blind box that unfolds into a brochure, paired with a character keychain as an identity artefact. Twelve information surfaces, deliberately configurable so Kita's youth beneficiaries can co-author their own advocacy materials.
Why blind box
The unboxing moment creates emotional memory; the object lives on a desk afterwards as a passive touchpoint. Culturally trendy, memorable on social, and produced cheaply at small batch sizes. Beats a leaflet on engagement lifecycle per unit distributed.
Research
Competitive SWOT (Praxium, Trybe); stakeholder interview with Kita board member Jim See; donor segmentation via GiveAsia & NVPC data; copy audit that banned "empower" in favour of Kita's preferred vocabulary of agency and co-creation.
Impact targets
Delivered with a mixed-methods evaluation plan — pre/post surveys, engagement analytics, and 6–12 month longitudinal focus groups. Targets, not yet measured:
Confidence+40% Site traffic3× Study6–12 mo
Takeaway
The strongest idea in the project wasn't the box — it was designing for configurability. Leaving deliberate blanks turned the toolkit from a statement into a platform, and made Kita's youths co-authors of the story being told about them.
